Initiatives to reduce Air Pollution



  • When purchasing a new car, consider one that is the most efficient, lowest-polluting vehicle or even a zero-emission electric car.
  • Replace energy-hungry incandescent lights with compact florescent light bulbs.
  • Opt for a fan instead of air conditioning.
  • se a programmable thermostat and set it to 780 F in the summer and 680F in the winter
  • Use a surge protector for multiple appliances and turn it off when products are not in use./li>
  • Asks your energy supplier for a home audit and inquire about alternative energy solutions like solar or wind.
  • When ready to replace, look for Energy Star appliances.
  • Paint with a brush instead of a sprayer
  • Choose products that use recycled materials.
  • Store all solvents in airtight containers.
  • Use water based cleaning products that are labeled zero VOC.
  • Read up on organic gardening, composting, and energy efficiency
  • Support nearby stores and restaurants, especially ones that use organic local ingredients.
  • Check with local, state, and federal agencies for rebate offers on alternative energy programs, products, etc.
  • Read up on organic gardening, composting, and energy efficiency.
  • Print and photocopy on both sides of paper.
  • Dress for the weather and adjust layers before adjusting the thermostat.
  • Turn off office equipment, computers, printers and fax machine, after hours.
